  • Soil Health Based off of this source accessed June 12th, 2024

    Squeeze Test

    Take damp soil (not too wet) and squeeze then open hand.

    • If it falls apart, it's sandy -> probably low on nutrients and does not retain moisture.
    • If it doesn't fall apart, it's clay.
    • Otherwise, it's Loamy -> good: Video Link

    Drainage Test

    Dig 30cm³, put aside for acidity test. Fill with water.

    • Less than four hours to drain - good.
    • More -> improve drainage by either:
      • Building a raised bed for your garden.
      • Planting in raised rows.
      • Adding more organic material to make it loamy (if your soil composition is off).

    Worm Test

    Moist soil above 10 Celsius should have ~10 worms in 30cm³ if healthy. If less, need more compost.

    pH Test

    Alkaline soil ("sweet soil") has pH > 7

    • Contains sodium/calcium/magnesium

    Baking soda (Sodium Bicarbonate NaHCO3) is a base like all alkali substances.

    • Mix baking soda and water with soil -> the more it fizzes, the more acidic your soil is.

    Vinegar is acidic

    • Mix 2 tablespoons of soil with vinegar -> The more it fizzes, the more alkaline (basic) your soil is.

  • Nicotiana rustica (Wild Tobacco)

    Direct sow in spring, pressing into the surface of the soil. Keep the soil lightly moist until germination, which usually occurs within 10-20 days. Or start indoors 6-8 weeks before the last frost of spring. Keep seedlings moist until they become established.
